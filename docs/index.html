Anthracyclines induce global changes in cardiomyocyte chromatin accessibility that overlap with cardiovascular disease loci

Authors: E. Renee Matthews, Raodahtullah O. Abodunrin, John D. Hurley, Jose A. Gutierrez, Michelle C. Ward

Figure 1 Thousands of chromatin accessibility changes are induced in iPSC-CMs after anthracycline treatment.

Figure 2 Early drug-dependent responsive chromatin regions are enriched in CpG-rich regions near transcription start sites.

Figure 3 Regions opening in response to drug treatment are enriched for transcription factor motifs including p53 and JunB.

Figure 4 Regions that open in response to drugs are enriched near signaling genes, while closing regions are enriched near cardiac genes.

Figure 5 Drug-induced chromatin accessibility changes correspond to changes in active histone modification and gene expression.

Figure 6 Drug responsive regions are near cardiotoxicity-associated SNPs.

Figure 7 Drug responsive regions overlap SNPs associated with atrial fibrillation and heart failure.
